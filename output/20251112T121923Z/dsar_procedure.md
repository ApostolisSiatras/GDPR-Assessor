# DSAR Handling Procedure
Last updated: 2025-11-12

## Purpose
This procedure outlines the steps to be taken when handling Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs) in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

## Intake & Verification
### Step 1: Receive and Verify Request
* Receive DSAR from data subject via email or mail
* Verify request is legitimate by checking for required information:
	+ Full name of data subject
	+ Contact details of data subject
	+ Description of the personal data being requested

## Assessment & Coordination
### Step 2: Identify Relevant Data
* Determine which departments and teams are involved in processing the data subject's personal data
* Identify relevant data controllers, processors, and third-party vendors

### Step 3: Assess Lawful Bases
* Review lawful bases for processing personal data (refer to ctx.lawful_bases)
	+ Legitimate interests
	+ Consent
	+ Contract
	+ Legal obligation
	+ Vital interests

## Response Content
### Step 4: Compile DSAR Response
* Provide a clear and concise response to the data subject, including:
	+ Description of personal data being processed
	+ Categories of personal data being processed (refer to ctx.sections.E.Rights)
	+ Recipients or categories of recipients of personal data (refer to ctx.processors)
	+ Transfers to third countries or international organizations (refer to ctx.transfers)
	+ Security measures in place to protect personal data (refer to ctx.security_measures)

## Timelines & Escalations
### Step 5: Establish Response Timeline
* Respond to DSAR within one month of receipt, unless extended by two months due to complexity

### Step 6: Escalate if Necessary
* If unable to respond within the timeframe, notify data subject and provide an estimated completion date
* Escalate to Data Protection Officer (DPO) or relevant department for assistance

## Recordkeeping
### Step 7: Maintain DSAR Records
* Keep a record of all DSARs received, including:
	+ Date of receipt
	+ Description of personal data requested
	+ Response provided to data subject
	+ Any subsequent correspondence
