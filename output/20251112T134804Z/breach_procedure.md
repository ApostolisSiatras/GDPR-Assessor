Here is the Markdown incident response runbook for personal data breaches:
```
# Personal Data Breach Procedure
Last updated: 2025-11-12

## Detection & Triage

1. **Initial Response**
	* Identify the breach through monitoring or notification from a third party (e.g., processor, transfer)
	* Document the time and date of discovery
2. **Triage**
	* Assess the severity of the breach using the risk rating (High Risk: 10.9%)
	* Determine if any personal data has been compromised (refer to retention policies)

## Containment

1. **Immediate Action**
	* Notify affected individuals through email or other communication channels
	* Take steps to prevent further unauthorized access (e.g., disable cookies, restrict access to sensitive data)
2. **Processor Notification**
	* Inform processors of the breach and request their assistance in containing the incident
3. **Transfer Notification**
	* If applicable, notify transfer recipients of the breach

## Assessment & Notification

1. **Breach Assessment**
	* Determine the scope of the breach (e.g., type of data compromised, number of individuals affected)
	* Review lawful bases for processing personal data to ensure compliance
2. **Notification**
	* Notify relevant authorities (e.g., Data Protection Authority) and affected individuals in accordance with applicable laws

## Communication

1. **Internal Notification**
	* Inform the organization's management and relevant stakeholders of the breach
	* Provide regular updates on the incident response efforts
2. **External Communication**
	* Respond to inquiries from affected individuals, processors, and transfer recipients
	* Maintain transparency throughout the incident response process

## Post-incident Review

1. **Root Cause Analysis**
	* Identify the root cause of the breach (e.g., security measure failure, human error)
	* Document lessons learned for future improvement
2. **Process Improvement**
	* Update policies and procedures to prevent similar breaches in the future
	* Conduct regular security audits to ensure compliance with applicable laws
```
