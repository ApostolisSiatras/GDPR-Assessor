# Personal Data Breach Procedure
Last updated: 2025-11-12

## Detection & Triage

When a personal data breach is suspected or detected:

1. **Notify the DPO**: Inform Dr Christos Kalloniatis, the Data Protection Officer (DPO), immediately.
2. **Gather information**:
	* Identify the source of the breach
	* Determine the type and scope of the breach
	* Assess the potential impact on individuals
3. **Activate incident response team**: Assemble a team to investigate and respond to the breach, including IT, security, and compliance personnel.
4. **Document the incident**: Record all relevant details, including the date, time, and description of the breach.

## Containment

To prevent further unauthorized access or data exfiltration:

1. **Isolate affected systems**:
	* Disconnect affected devices from the network
	* Disable accounts and credentials associated with the breach
2. **Secure sensitive data**:
	* Restrict access to sensitive data and systems
	* Implement temporary measures to protect against further breaches (e.g., encryption, firewalls)
3. **Notify processors and transfers**: Inform any third-party processors or transfer recipients about the breach.

## Assessment & Notification

To assess the impact of the breach and notify affected parties:

1. **Conduct a thorough investigation**:
	* Determine the root cause of the breach
	* Assess the scope and severity of the breach
2. **Notify affected individuals**: Inform individuals whose personal data has been compromised, in accordance with applicable laws (e.g., GDPR Article 34).
3. **Notify regulatory authorities**: Report the breach to relevant regulatory bodies, such as the ICO.
4. **Assess lawful bases**: Review the lawful bases for processing personal data and determine if any need to be updated or revised.

## Communication

To maintain transparency and inform stakeholders:

1. **Internal communication**:
	* Inform employees about the breach and its impact
	* Provide guidance on how to respond to affected individuals
2. **External communication**:
	* Publish a public notice about the breach (if required by law)
	* Respond to media inquiries and public concerns

## Post-incident Review

To identify lessons learned and improve incident response:

1. **Conduct a thorough review**: Analyze the breach, including its causes, impact, and response.
2. **Identify areas for improvement**:
	* Update policies and procedures
	* Implement additional security measures (e.g., encryption, access controls)
3. **Document lessons learned**: Record findings and recommendations to improve incident response.
