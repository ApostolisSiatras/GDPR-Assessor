Here is the Markdown incident response runbook for personal data breaches:
```markdown
# Personal Data Breach Procedure
Last updated: 2025-11-12

## Detection & Triage

When a potential personal data breach is detected, follow these steps:

1. **Notify the DPO**: Inform the Data Protection Officer (DPO) immediately.
2. **Gather information**:
	* Identify the source of the breach
	* Determine the type and scope of the breach
	* Assess the risk level
3. **Triage the incident**: Evaluate the severity of the breach and prioritize containment efforts.

## Containment

To prevent further damage, follow these steps:

1. **Isolate affected systems**:
	* Disconnect affected systems from the network
	* Disable access to sensitive data
2. **Notify processors and transfers**:
	* Inform any third-party processors or transfer recipients about the breach
3. **Review retention policies**: Check if any personal data is retained in accordance with our retention policies.

## Assessment & Notification

To assess the impact of the breach, follow these steps:

1. **Conduct a thorough investigation**:
	* Identify the root cause of the breach
	* Determine the extent of the breach
2. **Notify affected individuals**: Inform those whose personal data has been compromised.
3. **Assess lawful bases**: Review our lawful bases for processing to ensure compliance.

## Communication

To maintain transparency and trust, follow these steps:

1. **Publish a breach notification**:
	* On our website (if applicable)
	* To the relevant authorities
2. **Notify affected individuals again**: Provide an update on any further actions taken.
3. **Review cookies and security measures**:
	* Check if cookies are enabled and if they comply with regulations
	* Review our security measures to prevent similar breaches in the future.

## Post-incident Review

To improve our incident response, follow these steps:

1. **Conduct a post-incident review**: Identify lessons learned and areas for improvement.
2. **Update procedures and policies**: Reflect any changes made as a result of the breach.
3. **Review security measures**:
	* Check if our security measures are adequate to prevent similar breaches
	* Update our security measures as necessary.
```
