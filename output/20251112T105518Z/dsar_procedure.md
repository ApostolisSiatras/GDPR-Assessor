# DSAR Handling Procedure
Last updated: 2025-11-12

## Purpose
This procedure outlines the steps to be taken when handling Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs) in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

## Intake & Verification
1. Identify and verify the requestor's identity.
2. Confirm that the request is a DSAR under GDPR.
3. Check if the request is time-barred or has been made previously.

## Assessment & Coordination
1. Determine the lawful basis for processing the data in question (refer to ctx: `lawful_bases`).
2. Identify any processors involved in the processing of the data (refer to ctx: `processors`).
3. Assess if any transfers of personal data have taken place (refer to ctx: `transfers`).
4. Check retention periods for the requested data (refer to ctx: `retention`).

## Response Content
1. Provide a clear and concise response to the requestor.
2. Include all relevant information about the processing activities, including:
	* Lawful basis (refer to ctx: `lawful_bases`)
	* Processors involved (refer to ctx: `processors`)
	* Transfers of personal data (refer to ctx: `transfers`)
	* Retention periods (refer to ctx: `retention`)
3. Ensure the response is accurate, complete, and provided in a format that is easily accessible.

## Timelines & Escalations
1. Respond to DSARs within 30 days of receipt.
2. Escalate complex or high-priority requests to the Data Protection Officer (DPO) for further assistance.
3. Keep the requestor informed of any delays or issues with their request.

## Recordkeeping
1. Maintain a record of all DSARs received, including:
	* Request details
	* Response provided
	* Any subsequent communications
2. Ensure records are accurate, complete, and securely stored.
