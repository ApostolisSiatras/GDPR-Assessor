## Lawful Bases and Transparency

In accordance with Article 5 of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), which sets out the principles for processing personal data, we have identified the lawful bases for our processing activities. Specifically, Article 6 outlines the conditions under which personal data can be processed, including consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, and legitimate interests. In this section, we outline the lawful bases declared in our organization's context.

* **Consent**: We apply the Consent basis when a data subject has explicitly provided their consent for processing of their personal data.
* **Contract**: The Contract basis is applied when processing is necessary for the performance of a contract to which the data subject is party or in order to take steps at the request of the data subject prior to entering into a contract.
* **Legal Obligation**: We rely on the Legal Obligation basis when processing is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation to which our organization is subject.
* **Vital Interests**: The Vital Interests basis is applied when processing is necessary in order to protect the vital interests of the data subject or another natural person.

## Data Subject Rights

As a data subject, you have various rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) that enable you to control your personal data and how it is processed. At Redacted Organisation, we respect these rights and provide below an overview of what they entail.

### Core GDPR Rights

The core rights provided by the GDPR include:

- **Access**: The right to access your personal data held by us.
- **Rectification**: The right to correct any inaccuracies in your personal data.
- **Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)**: The right to request erasure of your personal data under certain conditions.
- **Restriction of Processing**: The right to restrict the processing of your personal data, for example if you contest its accuracy or object to its use.
- **Portability**: The right to receive a copy of your personal data in a structured and commonly used format.
- **Objection**: The right to object to the processing of your personal data for direct marketing purposes or other legitimate interests.
- **Automated Decisions**: The right not to be subject to automated decision-making, including profiling.

### Additional Rights

We also provide additional rights as part of our commitment to transparency and accountability:

- **Right to Data Portability**: You have the right to request a copy of your personal data in a structured format.
- **Right to Object to Profiling**: If we use your personal data for profiling or automated decision-making, you can object to this processing.

## Security and Incident Response

To ensure the protection of personal data in accordance with Article 32 of the GDPR, we have implemented various security measures to prevent unauthorized access, use, or disclosure of personal data. These measures are designed to be proportionate to the risks associated with our processing activities.

Preventive controls are essential in maintaining a secure environment for data processing. We have identified several key areas where preventive measures can be taken:

* Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments will be conducted to identify potential weaknesses in our systems.
* Access controls, including multi-factor authentication, will be implemented to ensure that only authorized personnel can access sensitive data.
* Data encryption will be used to protect personal data both in transit and at rest.

Detective controls are equally important in identifying and responding to security incidents. We have implemented the following detective measures:

* Log monitoring and analysis tools will be used to detect potential security breaches or unauthorized access attempts.
* Incident response plans will be developed and regularly tested to ensure that we can respond promptly and effectively in the event of a breach.

In the event of a data breach, we will follow the procedures outlined in Articles 33/34 of the GDPR. This includes:

1. **Notification**: We will notify the relevant authorities (e.g., Data Protection Authority) within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach.
2. **Assessment**: We will conduct an assessment to determine the likelihood and potential impact of the breach, including identifying affected individuals and data categories.
3. **Communication**: We will communicate with affected individuals, providing them with information about the breach, the measures we are taking to mitigate its effects, and any steps they can take to protect themselves.

When a breach involves personal data transferred to processors or third parties, we will also notify these entities in accordance with our contracts and agreements. For example:

* If a breach occurs due to a failure by one of our processors to implement adequate security measures, we will work closely with the processor to rectify the situation and ensure that affected individuals are notified.
* In cases where personal data is transferred to third parties for processing, we will notify these entities in accordance with our contracts and agreements, and take steps to mitigate any potential harm.
