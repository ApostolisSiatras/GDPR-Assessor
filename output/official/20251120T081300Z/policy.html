Data Subject Rights

As a data subject, you have various rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) that are designed to give you control over your personal data. At Redacted Organisation, we respect and support these rights, which include:

  • The right of access: You can request access to your personal data that we hold.
  • The right of rectification: If you believe any of the information we hold about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you can ask us to correct it.
  • The right of erasure: In certain circumstances, you have the right to request that we delete your personal data.
  • The right of restriction: You may also request that we restrict our processing of your data in specific situations.
  • The right of portability: If you want a copy of your personal data or if you'd like us to transfer it directly to another organisation, you can ask us for this.
  • The right of objection: You have the right to object to certain types of processing, such as direct marketing.
  • The right not to be subject to automated decision-making, including profiling.

In addition to these core rights, we also offer:

  • The right to data protection by design and default (ensured through our DPIA process)
  • The right to have your personal data processed in a secure manner (protected by our security measures)

If you wish to exercise any of these rights, please follow the procedure below:

  1. Identify which right(s) you would like to exercise.
  2. Contact our DPO via the published privacy email with your request. Please include your name and contact details so we can verify your identity and process your request efficiently.

We will respond to your request within one month of receiving it, unless the request is complex or voluminous in which case we may extend this period by up to two months. We will inform you if an extension is necessary and explain our reasons for doing so.

Once we have received your request, we will verify your identity before taking any action. This is a necessary step to ensure that only authorised individuals can access or modify their personal data.

If you need help with submitting a request or would like more information about the rights mentioned above, contact details are available upon request.

Security and Incident Response

To ensure the security of personal data in our possession, we have implemented various preventive controls to mitigate potential risks. These measures include:

  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments to identify and address potential weaknesses in our systems and processes.
  • Implementation of encryption for sensitive data both in transit and at rest, ensuring that even if unauthorized access is gained, the data remains unreadable.
  • Secure authentication and authorization protocols are in place to prevent unauthorized access to our systems and data.

In addition to these preventive controls, we have also implemented detective controls to quickly identify and respond to potential security incidents. These measures include:

  • Real-time monitoring of system logs and network activity to detect potential security breaches or anomalies.
  • Implementation of intrusion detection and prevention systems to quickly identify and block potential threats.
  • Regular penetration testing and red teaming exercises to simulate real-world attacks and identify vulnerabilities.

In the event of a data breach, we will follow the procedures outlined in Articles 33/34 of the GDPR. This includes:

  • Notifying the relevant authorities within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, including the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) and any affected individuals.
  • Conducting an investigation into the cause of the breach and implementing measures to prevent similar incidents from occurring in the future.
  • Cooperating with processors and transfers involved in the breach, as necessary.

We will also maintain a record of all data breaches, including the date and time of the breach, the type of personal data affected, and the measures taken to address the breach. This information will be used to improve our security measures and prevent similar incidents from occurring in the future.

Breach Response Timeline:

  1. Notification: Within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach, we will notify the relevant authorities and any affected individuals.
