Lawful Bases and Transparency

As per Article 5 of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), personal data must be processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently. This principle is further elaborated upon in Article 6, which outlines the specific lawful bases for processing personal data. In accordance with these principles, PRIVACT P.C has identified the following lawful bases for processing personal data:

  • Consent: Applied when an individual provides explicit consent to process their personal data.
  • Contract: Applied when processing is necessary for the performance of a contract to which the data subject is a party.
  • Legal Obligation: Applied when processing is necessary to comply with a legal obligation to which PRIVACT P.C is subject.

Please note that these lawful bases are declared, but not all may be applicable in every situation. Additional lawful bases may be identified and documented prior to processing personal data.

Regarding transparency, PRIVACT P.C aims to provide clear and concise information about its data collection practices through privacy notices. Data Subject Rights

As a data subject, you have several rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) that are designed to give you control over your personal data. At PRIVACT P.C., we respect these rights and provide you with information on how to exercise them.

Core GDPR Rights

The following core rights are enabled for all data subjects:

  • Right of access: You have the right to request a copy of your personal data that we hold.
  • Right of rectification: If you believe any of your personal data is inaccurate or incomplete, you can ask us to correct it.
  • Right of erasure (also known as the "right to be forgotten"): In certain circumstances, you can ask us to delete your personal data.
  • Right of restriction: You have the right to request that we restrict our processing of your personal data in specific situations.
  • Right of portability: If you want to transfer your personal data to another organization, we will provide it to you in a machine-readable format.
  • Right to object: You can object to us processing your personal data for direct marketing purposes or other legitimate interests.
  • Right not to be subject to automated decisions: We do not use automated decision-making processes that significantly affect individuals.

Additional Rights

In addition to the core GDPR rights, we also offer the following additional rights:

  • Right of notification in case of a data breach (enabled through our security measures)
  • Right to request information on the processing activities we carry out (available upon request)

Submitting Requests

If you would like to exercise any of your rights, please follow these steps:

  1. Contact our DPO via the published privacy email: apostolissiatras@gmail.com
  2. Provide clear details about which right you wish to exercise and what specific data you are referring to (if applicable)

Breach Response Timeline

In the event of a personal data breach, we will follow a structured response timeline to minimize the impact on affected individuals and comply with relevant regulations. This timeline includes:

  • Notification: We will notify the Data Protection Officer (DPO) and other relevant stakeholders within 24 hours of discovering a potential breach.
  • Assessment: The DPO will conduct an initial assessment of the breach, including its scope, severity, and potential impact on affected individuals.
  • Containment: We will take prompt action to contain or mitigate the breach, including isolating affected systems or data, notifying processors or transfers as necessary (e.g., Article 33(2)).
  • Communication: We will communicate with affected individuals, providing them with information about the breach, its impact on their personal data, and any steps they can take to protect themselves.
  • Reporting: We will report the breach to the relevant authorities, including the Hellenic Data Protection Authority (Article 33(1)).
  • Review: We will conduct a thorough review of the breach, identifying root causes and implementing corrective actions to prevent similar incidents in the future.
