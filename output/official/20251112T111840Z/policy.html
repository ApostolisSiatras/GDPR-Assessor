Lawful Bases and Transparency

In accordance with Article 5 of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), which emphasizes the importance of lawfulness, fairness, and transparency in data processing, Privact P.C. has identified the lawful bases for its processing activities. As per Article 6, these bases are essential to ensure that personal data is processed in a manner that respects individuals' rights and freedoms.

The following lawful bases have been declared:

  • Consent: Applied when an individual provides explicit consent for the collection and processing of their personal data.
  • Contract: Applied when processing is necessary for the performance of a contract or agreement to which the individual is a party.
  • Legal Obligation: Applied when processing is necessary to comply with a legal obligation to which Privact P.C. is subject.

Please note that if no lawful basis has been declared, it will be documented prior to processing.

In accordance with our transparency obligations, we provide information about our cookie practices and privacy notices. As cookies are currently disabled on our website due to the "no_website" reason, we do not collect or store any data through cookies at this time. However, when cookies are enabled in the future, we will use them for legitimate purposes such as remembering user preferences and improving user experience.

We supply data subject information through various means, including our website and direct communication channels. We also handle rights requests in accordance with Article 12 of the GDPR, which outlines the rights of individuals to access, rectify, erase, restrict processing, object to processing, and data portability.

By implementing these measures, Privact P.C. demonstrates its commitment to transparency and accountability in data processing activities.

Data Subject Rights

As a data subject, you have various rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) that enable you to control how your personal data is processed. At Privact P.C., we respect and support these rights, ensuring transparency in our operations.

Enabled Rights

Based on our security measures and organizational policies, the following rights are enabled for you:

  • Access: You have the right to request access to your personal data that we hold.
  • Rectification: If you believe any of your personal data is inaccurate or incomplete, you can request rectification.
  • Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): In certain circumstances, you may request us to erase your personal data.
  • Restriction: You have the right to restrict our processing of your personal data in specific situations.
  • Portability: If applicable, you can request a copy of your personal data for transfer to another controller.
  • Objection: You can object to the processing of your personal data based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.
  • Automated Decisions: We do not engage in automated decision-making processes that significantly affect individuals.

Submitting Requests

If you wish to exercise any of these rights, please follow this procedure:

  1. Identify Your Right: Clearly specify which right you are exercising and provide details about the personal data you are requesting.
  2. Contact Us: Reach out to us via email at info@privact.eu or by phone at +30 6940521952. Our Data Protection Officer (DPO), Christos Kalloniatis, will guide you through the process.
  3. Provide Information: Be prepared to provide any additional information we may need to verify your identity and locate the data in question.

Response Timelines

We aim to respond to all requests within one month from receipt of a valid request. In cases where it is necessary to extend this period, we will inform you within the initial month with reasons for the delay and an estimated completion date.

Verification Steps

To ensure your personal data is accurate and up-to-date, we may need to verify your identity before processing your request. This verification process is in place to protect both you and us from potential misuse of our services.

Contact Instructions

Breach Response Timeline

In the event of a personal data breach, we will follow the procedures outlined in Articles 33/34 of the GDPR. This includes:

  • Notifying the relevant authorities (e.g., Hellenic Data Protection Authority) within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, as required by Article 33.
  • Conducting an investigation into the cause and extent of the breach, including any potential impact on data subjects.
  • Notifying affected data subjects without undue delay, unless the personal data breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, as per Article 34.

We will also notify our processors and transfers, where applicable, to ensure that they take necessary measures to mitigate any potential harm. For example, if we engage with third-party vendors for data processing, we will notify them promptly in the event of a breach to enable them to take corrective action.
