Specifically, we rely on Article 6, which outlines the six lawful bases for processing personal data: Consent, Contract, Legal Obligation, Vital Interests, Public Task, and Legitimate Interests.

The following lawful bases have been declared:

  • Consent: We obtain explicit consent from data subjects before collecting or processing their personal data.
  • Contract: Personal data is processed in accordance with contractual obligations between our organization and the data subject.
  • Legal Obligation: We process personal data to comply with legal requirements, such as tax laws and regulations.
  • Vital Interests: In exceptional circumstances, we may process personal data to protect vital interests of the data subject or another individual.
  • Public Task: Our organization processes personal data in the public interest, such as for law enforcement purposes.
  • Legitimate Interests: We process personal data where it is necessary for our legitimate business interests and does not harm the rights and freedoms of the data subject.

Data Subject Rights

As a data subject, you have several rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) that enable you to control how your personal data is processed. These rights are designed to protect your privacy and ensure that your data is handled fairly and lawfully.

Core GDPR Rights

The following core rights are guaranteed by the GDPR:

  1. Access: The right to request access to your personal data, including information about how it's being used.
  2. Rectification: The right to correct any inaccuracies in your personal data.
  3. Erasure: The right to have your personal data deleted when it's no longer needed or if you withdraw consent.
  4. Restriction: The right to limit the processing of your personal data under certain circumstances.
  5. Portability: The right to transfer your personal data from one organization to another in a machine-readable format.
  6. Objection: The right to object to the processing of your personal data for direct marketing or other purposes.
  7. Automated Decisions: The right to object to decisions made solely by automated means.

Additional Rights

Security and Incident Response

Preventive and Detective Controls

To ensure the security of personal data, our organization has implemented various preventive and detective controls. These measures are designed to mitigate potential risks and prevent unauthorized access or breaches. For instance, we have established a robust password policy that requires all employees to use strong and unique passwords for accessing sensitive systems and data. Additionally, we conduct regular vulnerability assessments and penetration testing to identify and address any weaknesses in our security posture.

In the event of a suspected breach, our detective controls will be triggered, allowing us to quickly detect and respond to potential incidents. Our incident response plan is designed to minimize the impact of a breach and ensure that all necessary steps are taken to contain and remediate the issue. If a breach occurs, we will notify the relevant authorities and affected individuals within the required timeframe, as outlined in Articles 33/34 of the GDPR.

Breach Response Timeline

In the event of a confirmed data breach, our organization will follow a structured response timeline to ensure that all necessary steps are taken to contain and remediate the issue. This process will involve:

  • Immediate notification to the relevant authorities and affected individuals (Article 33)
