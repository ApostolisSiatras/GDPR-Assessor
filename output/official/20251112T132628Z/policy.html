Lawful Bases and Transparency

In accordance with Article 5 of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), which sets out the principles for processing personal data, we have identified the lawful bases upon which we rely to process your personal information. Specifically, we are guided by the principles outlined in Article 6, which permits processing where it is necessary for the performance of a contract, or when there is a legitimate interest that overrides individual rights and freedoms.

The following lawful bases have been declared:

  • Consent: We apply this basis when you provide explicit consent to process your personal data for specific purposes.
  • Contract: This basis is applied when we need to process your personal data in order to perform our contractual obligations to you.
  • Legal Obligation: We rely on this basis when processing your personal data is necessary to comply with a legal or regulatory requirement.

Please note that the lawful bases listed above are those that have been declared at the time of drafting this policy. If any additional lawful bases are identified, they will be documented prior to processing.

Regarding transparency and cookie practices, we want to assure you that we strive to provide clear and concise information about our data collection and processing activities. However, as cookies are currently disabled on our website due to the "no_website" reason, no personal data is being collected through this means at present. Nevertheless, when cookies are enabled in the future, we will ensure that all necessary transparency measures are taken, including providing clear information about the types of cookies used and their purposes.

Data subjects can find more information about our processing activities by contacting us directly or reviewing our privacy notices. We also handle data subject rights in accordance with Article 12 of the GDPR, which includes the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, object to, and transfer your personal data. Data Subject Rights

As a data subject, you have various rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) that enable you to control how your personal data is processed. At PRIVACT, we respect these rights and provide a framework for exercising them.

Enabled Rights

The following rights are enabled in our organization:

  • Access: You have the right to access your personal data that we hold.
  • Rectification: If your personal data is inaccurate or incomplete, you can request us to correct it.
  • Erasure: In certain circumstances, you may request us to erase your personal data. This includes when the data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected.
  • Restriction: You have the right to restrict our processing of your personal data in specific situations.
  • Portability: We provide a mechanism for you to obtain and reuse your personal data for your own purposes across different services.
  • Objection: If we are relying on legitimate interests as the legal basis for processing, you can object to that processing. You also have the right to object if we are processing your personal data for direct marketing purposes.
  • Automated Decisions: We do not make automated decisions about you using your personal data.

Submitting Requests

If you wish to exercise any of these rights, please follow this procedure:

  1. Write to us at Email: Dr Christos Kalloniatis with the subject line "Data Subject Rights Request".
  2. Clearly state which right(s) you are exercising and provide any necessary details.
  3. We will respond within one month of receiving your request, unless the request is complex or voluminous in which case we may take up to three months.

Response Timelines and Verification

We aim to process all requests as soon as possible. In some cases, we might need additional time to verify the identity of the person making the request or to locate the data in question. We will keep you informed about the progress of your request and let you know if there are any issues.
