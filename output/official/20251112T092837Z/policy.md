## Data Subject Rights

As a data subject, you have the following rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

* Access: The right to obtain confirmation as to whether or not personal data concerning you is being processed, and access to such data.
* Rectification: The right to rectify inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
* Erasure: The right to erasure of personal data that is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected.
* Restriction: The right to restrict processing of personal data in certain circumstances.
* Portability: The right to receive a copy of your personal data in a machine-readable format.
* Objection: The right to object to processing of personal data based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.
* Automated decisions: The right not to be subject to automated decision-making, including profiling.

## Security and Incident Response

### Preventive and Detective Controls

Our organization is committed to maintaining a robust security posture to protect personal data. While our current security measures are limited, we recognize the importance of implementing proportionate controls to mitigate potential risks.

We commit to implementing the following preventive and detective controls:

* Regular vulnerability assessments and penetration testing
* Implementation of encryption for sensitive data in transit and at rest
* Secure access controls, including multi-factor authentication and least privilege access
* Monitoring and logging of security-related events
* Incident response planning and training

### Breach Response Timeline

In the event of a personal data breach, we will follow the procedures outlined below:

1. **Detection**: Our organization will detect a potential breach through monitoring and incident response activities.
2. **Notification**: We will notify the relevant authorities (e.g., Data Protection Authority) within 72 hours of detection, as required by Article 33 GDPR.
3. **Assessment**: We will conduct an assessment to determine the scope and impact of the breach.
4. **Communication**: We will communicate with affected individuals and stakeholders, providing them with information about the breach, its causes, and any measures taken to mitigate its effects (Article 34 GDPR).
5. **Remediation**: We will take steps to remediate the breach, including implementing additional security controls as necessary.
