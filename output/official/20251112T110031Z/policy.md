## Lawful Bases and Transparency

In accordance with Article 5 of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), which sets out the principles for processing personal data, we have identified the lawful bases that justify our processing activities. Specifically, Article 6 outlines the conditions under which personal data may be processed, including consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, and legitimate interests. In this section, we outline the lawful bases declared in our organization's context.

* **Consent**: We apply the Consent basis when an individual provides explicit permission for us to process their personal data.
* **Contract**: The Contract basis is applied when processing is necessary for the performance of a contract to which the data subject is party or in order to take steps at the request of the data subject prior to entering into a contract.
* **Legal Obligation**: We rely on the Legal Obligation basis when processing is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation to which our organization is subject.

Note that no lawful bases have been declared beyond those listed above. However, we will document any additional bases prior to commencing processing activities.

In addition to these lawful bases, we also provide transparency regarding our cookie practices. As cookies are currently disabled on our website due to the "no_website" reason, no data is collected through this means. When cookies are enabled in the future, we will ensure that clear and concise information about our use of cookies is provided to data subjects, including details on how to manage their preferences.

## Data Subject Rights

As a data subject, you have several rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) that are designed to give you control over your personal data. At Privact P.C., we respect and support these rights, which include:

* The right of access: You can request information about the personal data we hold about you.
* The right of rectification: If you believe any of your personal data is inaccurate or incomplete, you can ask us to correct it.
* The right of erasure (also known as the "right to be forgotten"): In certain circumstances, you can request that we delete your personal data.
* The right of restriction: You can ask us to restrict our processing of your personal data in specific situations.
* The right of portability: If you want a copy of your personal data or if you'd like it transferred to another organization, we'll help facilitate this.
* The right to object: You have the right to object to certain types of processing, such as direct marketing.
* The right not to be subject to automated decisions: We will inform you if any decision is made about you using automated processes.

In addition to these core rights, our organization has implemented various security measures (listed below) that further protect your data. These include:

* Data encryption
* Access controls and authentication mechanisms
* Regular security audits and penetration testing

If you wish to exercise any of the above-mentioned rights or have questions about our data protection practices, please follow these steps:

1. Review our contact information: You can find our contact details on our website (info@privact.eu) or by contacting us via phone (+30 6940521952).
2. Submit your request: Please provide clear and specific details about the right you wish to exercise and any supporting documentation.
3. Wait for a response: We will respond to your request within one month, as required by GDPR regulations.

Please note that we may need additional time to process complex requests, but we will always inform you of any delays and provide an estimated timeline for completion. Once we've received your request, our Data Protection Officer (DPO) will verify the details and ensure a thorough investigation is conducted.

If you have any questions or concerns about this policy or would like more information on how to exercise your rights, please don't hesitate to contact us. Our DPO is available to answer any queries you may have, so feel free to reach out via email (info@privact.eu) or phone (+30 6940521952). Contact details are also available upon request if you'd prefer not to share sensitive information publicly.

We're committed to transparency and will always strive to provide clear and concise responses to your requests. Thank you for entrusting us with your personal data, and we look forward to working with you to ensure the protection of your rights under GDPR.
