Data Subject Rights

As a data subject, you have various rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) that enable you to control how your personal data is processed. At PRIVACT, we respect these rights and provide you with information on how to exercise them.

Core GDPR Rights

The following are the core rights granted by the GDPR:

  1. Right of Access: You have the right to request access to your personal data that we hold.
  2. Right to Rectification: If your personal data is inaccurate or incomplete, you can request us to rectify it.
  3. Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): In certain circumstances, you may request us to erase your personal data.
  4. Right to Restriction of Processing: You have the right to restrict our processing of your personal data in specific situations.
  5. Right to Data Portability: We will provide you with a copy of your personal data in a structured and commonly used format, allowing you to transfer it to another controller.
  6. Right to Object: In certain circumstances, you can object to the processing of your personal data for direct marketing purposes or other legitimate interests.
  7. Right Not to Be Subject to Automated Decision-Making: You have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing.

Additional Rights

In addition to the core GDPR rights, we also offer:

  1. Data Protection by Design and Default: We implement data protection principles in our processes and systems.
  2. Data Minimization: We only collect and process personal data that is necessary for specific purposes.
  3. Transparency: We provide clear information about how your personal data will be used.

Security and Incident Response

Preventive and Detective Controls

To ensure the security of personal data, PRIVACT has implemented various preventive and detective controls. These measures are designed to prevent unauthorized access or breaches, as well as detect any potential incidents in a timely manner. For instance, we have established strict access controls, ensuring that only authorized personnel can access sensitive data. Additionally, our systems are regularly updated with the latest security patches, and our network is protected by robust firewalls and intrusion detection systems.

However, it's worth noting that our current list of security measures is empty. In light of this, we will implement proportional controls to ensure the protection of personal data. This may include conducting regular vulnerability assessments, implementing encryption for sensitive data, and providing training to employees on data protection best practices.

Breach Response Timeline

In the event of a breach, PRIVACT will follow a structured timeline to respond promptly and effectively. As per Article 33 of the GDPR, we will notify the relevant authorities and affected individuals within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach. This notification will include details about the nature of the breach, the number of individuals affected, and any measures taken to mitigate its impact.

We will also conduct a thorough investigation into the cause of the breach, identifying any weaknesses in our systems or processes that may have contributed to it. If necessary, we will notify our processors and transfers partners, as per Article 34, to ensure they are aware of the situation and can take appropriate action.
