# DSAR Handling Procedure
Last updated: 2025-11-12

## Purpose
This procedure outlines the steps to be taken when handling Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs) in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

## Intake & Verification
1. Identify and acknowledge receipt of a DSAR from the data subject.
2. Verify the identity of the data subject and confirm their request is legitimate.
3. Check if the request is complete, including all necessary information and documentation.

## Assessment & Coordination
1. Determine the lawful basis for processing the personal data requested (refer to ctx: "lawful_bases").
2. Identify any processors involved in the processing of the personal data (refer to ctx: "processors").
3. Assess whether any transfers of personal data have taken place, including to third countries (refer to ctx: "transfers").
4. Determine the retention period for the requested data (refer to ctx: "retention").

## Response Content
1. Provide a clear and concise response to the DSAR, including:
	* A description of the personal data processed.
	* The purposes for which the data was collected and processed.
	* Any third parties with whom the data has been shared.
	* Information on the safeguards in place to protect the data (refer to ctx: "safeguards").
2. Include any relevant documentation or evidence to support the response.

## Timelines & Escalations
1. Respond to DSARs within 30 calendar days of receipt, unless an extension is necessary due to complexity.
2. Escalate complex or high-priority requests to the Data Protection Officer (DPO) for assistance.

## Recordkeeping
1. Maintain a record of all DSARs received, including:
	* Date and time of receipt.
	* Details of the request.
	* Response provided.
2. Store records securely in accordance with data retention policies (refer to ctx: "retention").
